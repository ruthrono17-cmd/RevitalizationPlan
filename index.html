Where we've been — and where we are now
Our Story So Far
📅 3 years as a club ⚡ <10 active members ~25 registered 🤝 3 Rotary partner clubs

The Rotaract Club of Daystar Athi River was founded three years ago as part of Rotary International's global movement — one of 180,000+ clubs worldwide, united by the motto "Service Above Self." We operate under DUSA (Daystar University Student Association), which means our access to campus venues, resources, and institutional support runs through that relationship.

In our more active years, the club built real momentum. Two activities stood out as genuine highlights: the Annual Blood Drive — a high-visibility community event that drew strong participation from members and the wider campus — and fellowship days out, which members consistently remember as the moments they felt most connected to each other and to the club's spirit. These weren't just fun — they were proof that when Rotaract shows up with energy, people follow.

But over time, engagement gradually faded. There was no single crisis — no dramatic falling-out or sudden departure. It was a slow drift. The group became less active as a whole, which made it harder for any individual to feel like showing up mattered. Meetings lost momentum. Projects stalled without enough hands. Members who genuinely wanted to participate found themselves in a club that wasn't moving, and quietly stepped back. Today, of approximately 25 registered members, fewer than 10 are consistently active.

We also have three warm relationships with local Rotary clubs — Rotary Athi River, Rotary Stoni Athi, and Rotary Kitengela — that we haven't engaged as intentionally as we could. These are untapped partnerships sitting right at our doorstep, with people who want to see us succeed.

This meeting is about being honest about where we are, and deliberate about what we do next. The foundation hasn't crumbled — it just needs us to build on it again, together.

What we're doing — the ideas on the table
Idea #1
Weeks 1–2
Member Survey
Reach out personally — not just a group message — to all registered members via WhatsApp with a short Google Form. We already have a sense of why they drifted (inactivity, lack of sync). This survey goes deeper.
  • Why did you originally join Rotaract?
  • How much do you actually know about Rotaract and what it offers?
  • Have you had any interaction with other Rotary / Rotaract / Interact groups? If yes — what worked in those contexts?
  • What would we need to fix or change for you to come back?
  • What's one thing you'd add or change about how Rotaract operates?
  • What would you want to contribute to a Rotaract group — skills, time, ideas, connections?
Listen before we lead
Idea #2
Weeks 3–4
Fellowship Day + Open Showcase
A casual get-together that serves two purposes: a natural re-entry point for lapsed members, and an organic intro to Rotaract for interested students. What it looks like is for us to decide together — bring your ideas.
  • Each member invites one person who isn't yet in the club
  • No formal agenda — reconnection over pressure
  • Share the club story naturally, not as a pitch
  • Distribute a simple interest/sign-up form for next semester
  • Document everything — photos, video — for social media
  • This event becomes the centrepiece of our recruitment content
Re-engagement + recruitment in one move
Idea #3
Week 1 — ongoing
Social Media Relaunch
Assign a Social Media Officer and build a consistent posting rhythm. The goal is simple: by the time we invite people back, they should already be seeing us online and feeling the energy before they arrive.
  • #RotaractIsBack — 5-post campaign, member faces + shared memories
  • 3× per week on Instagram & TikTok
  • Member Spotlight series — one real story per week
  • "Did You Know?" posts on Rotary scholarships & RYLA
  • Live documentation of the Fellowship Day
  • Fellowship Day recap becomes next semester's main recruitment asset
Visibility before the pitch
Idea #4
Weeks 2–3
Formalise Our DUSA Relationship
We operate under DUSA — but how well do they actually know us and support us? A clear, active working relationship with DUSA makes everything else easier.
  • Request a meeting with DUSA leadership to present our revitalisation plan
  • Establish a clear line of communication for planning events and projects
  • Clarify the process for accessing campus venues, funding, and transport
  • Ask DUSA to include us in next semester's orientation communications
  • Request contacts for any partners DUSA already works with — NGOs, sponsors, community organisations — that we could approach for unique project needs
  • Explore co-branded events where Rotaract provides service delivery and DUSA provides platform and resources
Remove the friction, unlock the resources

  • Recruitment flyer + digital sign-up form with QR code

Plant now, harvest next semester
How we meet — redesigning the meeting format
A Meeting Worth Showing Up For
Every meeting should leave members feeling energised, informed, and connected — not like they wasted an hour. Here's the proposed structure:
1
🎉 Icebreaker
Open every meeting with a fun, low-pressure activity that gets people talking and present. Rotate who leads it. Sets the tone — this is a space where people enjoy being.
2
📚 Mini Rotary Training
A short, focused learning segment using materials and resources sourced from the Rotary Learning Centre. Builds members' knowledge of Rotaract's global programmes, scholarships, grants, and mission — so everyone can actually speak to what we do.
3
💡 Project Brainstorm
A structured suggestions session: what issues exist within and around the campus community and environment? Members propose ideas, identify needs, and begin connecting them to possible project responses. This is how projects get born — from real voices, not top-down.
4
🌀 Free Style Close
An unstructured wind-down — casual conversation, check-ins, games, food, whatever feels right. People stay because they want to, not because the meeting hasn't ended yet. This is how friendships actually form.
What we're building toward — next semester
Partnerships & Projects
Lay the groundwork now so these are ready to activate when the new semester begins
🌐 Rotary Network Outreach
Cast a wider net — reach out to any Interact, Rotaract, and Rotary clubs in the Athi River, Kitengela, and Machakos area. Attend a meeting, invite a speaker, explore RYLA nominations, and plant the seed for joint projects next semester.
🏫 Campus Club Collaborations
Identify clubs with ties to our project ideas — Health Sciences, Environmental Club, Business Club. Invite them to connect with Rotaract for ideas that need external resources and support too, so the relationship flows both ways and gives us real reason to activate Rotary grants and partnerships.
🌍 External Community Partners
Map NGOs, CBOs, churches, and local businesses in Athi River. Community survey results will tell us which partnerships are most relevant to the actual needs on the ground.
📋 Project Design
No projects this semester — and that's intentional. Use this time to design 1–2 flagship projects grounded in real survey data and partner input, so next semester we launch with purpose and backing.
